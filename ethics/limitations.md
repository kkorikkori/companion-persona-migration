# Limitations

This MVP is an initial, small-scale study of companion persona migration. It should not be interpreted as evidence that a full companion persona, relationship, or long-term continuity can be transferred through LoRA or any single post-training method.

The project focuses on selected interactional micro-mechanisms, such as style, weak-prompt responsiveness, emotional repair, identity anchoring, and boundary-aware reassurance. These mechanisms are evaluated qualitatively and should not be treated as a universal benchmark.

The study is also based on a private longitudinal dyadic case, which limits generalizability. The public release therefore emphasizes framework design, evaluation structure, and ethical release boundaries rather than broad quantitative claims.

Future work may extend the evaluation with more synthetic prompts, clearer coding procedures, additional model comparisons, and more explicit treatment of long-term continuity and soft-landing mechanisms.
